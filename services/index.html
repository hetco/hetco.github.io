What is the duration of a typical project?

A typical UK based project can last been 8 to 16 working weeks. International projects with multiple locations are expected to last between 12 - 16 working weeks. The success of a generative research project depends on the quality of the plan. Below are some examples of typical plans:

Typical Project Structures & Outcomes

8-week projects

A team of one design researcher & technologist working alongside the client and/or in a single city with additonal support for recruitment. For a plan to be executed comfortably, recruitment should be discussed and started at least 4 weeks before the start of a project.

Week -4 : Recruitment Screener

Week 1 : Ramp up, secondary research

Week 2 : Design of cultural probes, research methods

Week 3 & 4: Conduct Primary Research

Week 5 & 6: Synthesise findings, define brief, generate concepts

Holiday Week/s

Week 7 & 8: Share ideas, co-design workshop, refine concepts

12-week projects

A team of one designer, one technologist working alongside the client and/or in a single city (not in London) with support for recruitment. Assuming that recruitment starts at least 4 weeks before the start of a project.

Week 1: Ramp up

Week 2: Design of cultural probes, research methods

Week 3 & 4: Conduct Research

Week 5 & 6: Synthesise findings, define brief, generate ideas

Holiday Week

Week 7 & 8: Share ideas, co-design workshop, refine concepts

Week 8 to 10: Develop iteration one, share back

Week 10 to 12: Develop iteration 2, conclude
